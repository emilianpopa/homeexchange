Getting There

Car rental + overnight ferry — the perfect family combination

🚗

Recommendation

Car Rental

  • Pick up at Copenhagen Airport (CPH) — drop off same location on return
  • 7-seater SUV or minivan recommended (Volvo XC90, VW Multivan, or Ford Galaxy) — essential with pram, baby gear, and luggage for 23 nights
  • Book unlimited kilometres — Norway is big
  • Request child seats (infant seat + toddler seat) in advance — limited availability in summer
  • Take full comprehensive insurance — Norwegian mountain roads can be narrow
  • Ask for AutoPASS toll tag — most Norwegian tolls are cashless. Cost is ~€30–50 for the whole trip
  • Best companies: Hertz, Avis, Sixt (all at CPH airport). Budget ~€350–500/week for a 7-seater
⚠️ Book before May — 7-seaters sell out fast in summer
⛴️

Recommended route

Overnight Ferry

  • Fjord Line: Hirtshals (Denmark) → Bergen, Norway — daily departures
  • Journey: ~17 hours overnight — departs ~3pm, arrives Bergen ~8am next day
  • Driving to Hirtshals from Copenhagen: 3.5h — depart morning of Jul 14
  • Book a family cabin (4-berth) — small children sleep well on the rocking ship. Restaurant, kids' play room, deck views of fjords
  • Return: Bergen → Hirtshals is not needed — we drive back via Oslo. DFDS Oslo→Copenhagen ferry optional for return
  • Cost: ~€280–380 for car + 4 passengers + cabin (book early for best rates)
💡 Stop at Skagen (tip of Denmark) en route — where two seas meet, great for kids

Stops Not to Miss

Wild camping, iconic roads, and secret waterfalls

⛺ Wild camping spot

Lake Lesjaskogsvatnet

A long, serene glacial lake in the Gudbrandsdalen valley, right on the E136 return route from Trollstigen. Norway's right-to-roam law lets you pitch a tent anywhere 150m from a building. Several small cabins (hytter) bookable on Booking.com for a comfy option with the baby.

🌀

🏔 Iconic road

Trollstigen

Norway's most famous road — 11 hairpin bends clinging to the cliff face with a raging waterfall alongside. The visitor center at the top has a terrific café with valley views. Takes about 30 minutes to drive up slowly. Open May–October.

💧

💧 Roadside waterfall

Tvindefossen

One of Norway's most accessible and beautiful waterfalls — just pull off the road near Voss. The falls drop 152 metres in a single ribbon. A 5-minute walk from the car park brings you right to the base — spray on your face. Clara will be absolutely mesmerised.

🏕

⛵ Return overnight · Catherine's tip

Kilefjorden Camping

A stunning nature campsite in the Numedal valley with a small children's beach, sauna, great kitchen and bathroom facilities. Tiny deserted islands you can row out to — pick blueberries and fish straight off the boat. Perfect last-night-in-Norway magic for the whole family.

Family Travel Tips

Norway with a toddler and a newborn — what to know

🌞

Midnight Sun

In late July/August it barely gets dark in western Norway — sunset around 11pm, twilight until midnight. Bring blackout blinds for the kids' room. Incredible for evening walks.

🛣️

Driving in Norway

Roads are excellent but mountain routes can be single-lane with passing places. Speed limits 80 km/h outside towns. AutoPASS tag handles all toll roads automatically. Petrol stations every 30–50 km.

⛺

Allemannsretten

Norway's "right to roam" — you can legally camp anywhere in nature for up to 2 nights, as long as you're 150m from the nearest dwelling. Lakes, forests, mountains — all free. Bring a good tent or book a hytte (cabin).

👶

Baby & Toddler

Norway is extremely family-friendly. All restaurants have high chairs, most attractions are free under 3. Good baby changing facilities everywhere. Supermarkets (Kiwi, Rema 1000) stock familiar brands. Pack a carrier for mountain walks with Stella.

📱

park4night App

The essential app for wild camping in Norway — find lakeside spots, forest clearings, quiet bays, and free overnight parking with real reviews and photos. Essential for the 12-day free-roaming stretch between Bergen and Ulsteinvik. Available on iOS and Android.
